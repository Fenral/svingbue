Reel 01 · Spin

“Hitting up lowers your spin” — not by itself.

Sharpened: spin = spin loft (dynamic loft − attack angle). Attack angle IS in the equation — but chasing it fails because golfers add loft while hitting up, cancelling it. The reliable lever is dynamic loft.

HOOK0:00
The range myth
“Just hit UP on it to kill your spin.”
Sound familiar? ✕ Half-true at best.
Swipe → what actually controls spin.
REVEAL0:03
Spin loft = the gap
Spin follows this wedge — dynamic loft − attack.
PAYOFF0:07
The trap & the fix
Hit up + add loft → wedge unchanged.
Cut loft → wedge shrinks → spin drops.
Chase spin loft, not attack angle.

Caption

You don’t lower spin by hitting up — you lower it by shrinking your spin loft. Hit up and add loft, and the wedge never changes. The real lever is dynamic loft. Stop chasing attack angle. 🎯

Reel 02 · Curve

Your face can be OPEN to the target — and still hook.

Bulletproof as stated. Start line ≈ face. Curve ≈ face − path (never face − target). Face open to target but closed to path = hook.

HOOK0:00
The assumption
“Hooking it? Your face must be closed.”
✕ It can point right of your target and still hook.
Swipe → proof.
REVEAL0:03
Top-down · right-handed
Face right of target, path further right → face closed to path.
PAYOFF0:07
The law
Start = face.
Curve = face − path.
Your curve never signs toward your target — only your path.
Open to target ≠ open to path.

Caption

Your clubface can point right of your target and the ball still hooks. Why? Curve is set by face-to-path, never face-to-target. Face +2°, path +6° → the ball starts right and banana’s left. 🍌
