Per-card grass-top measurement (pixel-sampled, not reused from 08)

Each backdrop is 1376×768, unmodified, not overwritten, no image generation used. The SVG <image> crop box (x=-40 y=-40 w=1250 h=620, xMidYMid slice, onto the 1170×540 viewBox) maps source PNG row → viewBox Y via vbY = 0.9084·row − 78.82. Using that mapping, horizontal guide lines were rendered directly onto each source photo at candidate viewBox-Y values and visually inspected against the treeline / pole-base / grass-brightening cues, then confirmed numerically (green-minus-blue channel + brightness scans down multiple columns).

08 (clean): canonical card — treeline sits right at yH=312 / grass-band top 324 (unchanged from range-08-elevated-mock.html, already verified 5/5).
13 (wet): forest treeline reads noticeably higher in-frame than 08's (a small pin/target marker sits on the grass right where the canopy ends) — measured grass-band top ≈305, so yH was pushed up to 292 to keep the same ~3px verification margin as 08.
11 (tunnel): no readable forest silhouette — the light-pole corridor's own vanishing point (where the smallest, most distant poles plant into the ground) is the only reliable ground-plane cue, and it sits much deeper in-frame than either other photo — measured grass-band top ≈400, so yH is anchored at 394. This card's usable grass band is roughly half the vertical height of 08's/13's, so its perspective throw reads flatter — that is the photo's own geometry, not an inconsistency in the framing math.

yBottom=518 (foreground tee) is unchanged across all three — all three photos show a paved tee/mat apron starting in roughly the same place near the bottom edge of the frame.
